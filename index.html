What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime in which a target or targets are contacted by email, telephone, or text message by someone posing as a legitimate institution to lure individuals into providing sensitive data such as personally identifiable information, banking and credit card details, and passwords.

Common Types of Phishing Attacks

Spear Phishing

Highly targeted attacks aimed at a specific individual, business, or organization. Attackers customize their emails with the victim’s name and job title.

Whaling

A form of spear phishing aimed at high-profile targets like CEOs or CFOs to steal sensitive corporate information.

Smishing

Phishing that involves a text message (SMS). You might get a text saying your account is locked and you must click a link to unlock it.

Vishing

Voice phishing. Attackers use phone calls to trick victims into giving up personal information over the phone.

How to Recognize Phishing

1. Generic Greetings: "Dear Customer" instead of your actual name.
2. Suspicious Links: Hover over a link to see the real URL. If it looks strange, don't click.
3. Sense of Urgency: Threats of account closure or legal action.
4. Bad Grammar: Professional companies rarely have spelling mistakes in their official emails.

Safety Checklist

✔ Use MFA

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication on all sensitive accounts.

✔ Update Software

Keep your browser and operating system updated to the latest security versions.

✔ Think Before Clicking

Never click on links or download attachments from unknown senders.
